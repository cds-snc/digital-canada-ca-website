Help us design a service to report cybercrimes to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)

Have you, or anyone you know, ever been phished, hacked or called by a scammer? Whether or not you’ve been affected, you can help us fight cyber crimes and scams by participating in research.

We’d like to speak to Canadians about online crime and have you try out a reporting tool we’ve been developing with the RCMP. You don’t need to be a victim of a scam or have any previous web experience, but you will need to be able to access a website and participate in a video call.

If you would like to help us, or know someone else who can, please contact CDSResearch-SNCRecherche@tbs-sct.gc.ca for more information on how to be involved.

How you can help

We'd like to talk to you via video chat (zoom) while you use our website to get your feedback. The conversation shouldn't take longer than 45 minutes, and we can reach you at a time that's convenient for you.

By participating in this study, you understand that:

  • You are volunteering to participate. You can stop at any time for any reason, without any consequences.
  • Your information will be anonymous. This means your responses will not be linked to you.
  • Due to this, you will not be able to withdraw or correct your information once provided.
  • Please do not provide any information in your responses that would identify you.
  • Your participation and answers will not affect your access to Government of Canada services or benefits.

We'll use Zoom for the session. Here’s a link to their privacy policy: https://zoom.us/privacy

CDS and RCMP may collect your personal information. For example, your name and experience with cyber crime. To learn about how CDS and RCMP protect your personal information, please see the privacy statement, below.

For any further questions about this research, please contact:

Privacy Notice:

Participating is completely voluntary.

By participating, you consent that CDS and RCMP may write down the information listed above. Specifically, your name (only for consent), language, experience with cybercrime, and experience using the prototype.

CDS and RCMP will use this information to improve a service. This personal information will not be used for any “administrative purposes”. This means that it will not affect your access to Government of Canada services.

CDS and RCMP may share what we have learned from this research, including quotes, within government or with the public. Your name will not be linked with your responses, quotes, or narratives.
