<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How’s CDCP using GC Notify? Any benefits to share?</strong></h2>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Emailing service updates to verified contacts</strong></h3>



<p>Thanks to GC Notify, CDCP applicants can choose to engage with the program digitally vs letter mail, saving the program physical mailing costs. We’ve sent around 1.4 million automated new communication emails to CDCP applicants using the notification service and its <a href="https://documentation.notification.canada.ca/en/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">API feature</a>, and the number continues to grow as awareness of the service increases.</p>



<p><strong>CDCP sends 3 types of notifications:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Verification code: </strong>To ensure the email the client provided us is valid and can be used to communicate with them, an email containing a verification code is sent to that address.&nbsp; To complete the digital registration, the client must enter the verification code.</li>



<li><strong>Confirmation email:</strong> Lets the client know that their CDCP application has been successfully submitted.</li>



<li><strong>New communication:</strong> Advises clients that there is a new communication related to their CDCP application for viewing in their <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/services/my-account.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">My Service Canada Account (MSCA) account</a>. This can be related to application being processed, their determination has been processed, an invitation to renew, etc.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Benefits: Improved UX for applicants</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Verification process makes engagement easier:</strong> Ensuring that emails are verified through an authorization code allows ESDC to properly engage with CDCP clients and know that any correspondence is going to the correct accounts. This reduces the time it takes to determine contact info.</li>



<li><strong>Applicants are updated faster via email than letter mail:</strong> Improves user experiences (UX) with the program, and reduces the amount of follow-up requests to our Service Canada officers.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why did your team adopt GC Notify?</strong></h2>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Using the notification service addressed our need for digital comms:</strong> Prior to the introduction of GC Notify, most of the correspondence to CDCP clients was via letter mail. To drive a more digital-first solution, as of May 2024 clients now have the choice to view letters digitally, significantly reducing the costs associated with communication.&nbsp;</li>



<li><strong>Knowing other ESDC teams use it gave us confidence:</strong> We knew it was stable, reliable, easy to integrate, and familiar to us.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
