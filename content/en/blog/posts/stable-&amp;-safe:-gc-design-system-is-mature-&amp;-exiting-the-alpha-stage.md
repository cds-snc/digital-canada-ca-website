<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What does exiting the Alpha stage mean?</strong></h2>



<p>Exiting Alpha signals that GC Design System is in active use and continues to evolve:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>It’s safe to use in production, well-supported, and continuously improved.</strong> This represents ongoing development, validated components, and a foundation that stakeholders can rely on.</li>



<li><strong>It has reached a level of maturity where departments can begin planning for adoption with clear expectations. </strong>Stability includes a predictable versioning system, so teams can understand the impacts of changes before updating.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<details class="wp-block-details is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow"><summary>What exiting Alpha doesn’t mean</summary>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>It does not mean every component and pattern exists</strong>: We’re not offering every component or template ever used across the GC, nor those that existed in previous design systems. That has never been the goal. What we have built is the foundational layer of GC Design System, and our offering will continue to expand and improve over time.</li>



<li><strong>It does not mean breaking changes will never happen</strong>: Exiting Alpha means we will significantly reduce the frequency of breaking changes, but not eliminate them entirely. Our stable versioning strategies will help teams plan for and adapt to any breaking changes when they do occur.</li>



<li><strong>It does not mean everything must migrate from WET and GCWeb</strong>: Exiting alpha does not mean that GC Design System is immediately replacing WET or GCWeb. Teams can continue maintaining and building web experiences with the design system that best meets their needs. The future direction for design systems in the GC will continue to be communicated by GC Design System, in partnership with the Canada.ca Experience Office and Principal Publisher.</li>
</ul>
</details>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What can users expect from a stable GC Design System?</strong></h2>



<p>GC Design System is an official, production-ready design system for the Government of Canada and <a href="https://design-system.canada.ca/en/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">now has a new subdomain</a> to reflect the move out of its previous alpha phase. This change signals that GC Design System is mature, dependable, and continuously evolving with ongoing enhancements.&nbsp;</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Key GC Design System features</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>System foundations</strong>: <a href="https://design-system.alpha.canada.ca/en/styles/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">design tokens</a> for spacing, colour, and typography; <a href="https://design-system.alpha.canada.ca/en/components/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">web components</a>; and a <a href="https://design-system.alpha.canada.ca/en/css-shortcuts/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">CSS styling framework</a>.</li>



<li><strong>Users can build using the </strong><a href="https://design-system.alpha.canada.ca/en/start-to-use/develop/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>front-end framework of your choice</strong></a><strong>, or no framework at all:</strong> GC Design System offers developers the option to code in HTML, React, Vue, Angular, Next.JS (for SSR). Teams across government can use the same design system, regardless of their tech stack.</li>



<li><strong>Web and application designers can prototype with a comprehensive </strong><a href="https://www.figma.com/community/file/1128687821123298228" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>Figma library</strong></a>, rich with components and variables that are mirrored in code.</li>



<li><a href="https://design-system.alpha.canada.ca/en/accessibility/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>Accessibility</strong></a>: GC Design System, including all its components, meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 AA.</li>



<li><a href="https://design-system.alpha.canada.ca/en/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>Everything from code, design, and guidance lives in one place</strong></a><strong>:</strong> ensures everyone works from the same source of truth.</li>
</ul>



<details class="wp-block-details is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow"><summary>What&#8217;s included in this release?</summary>
<p><strong>New code packages</strong> <strong>have been released to:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ensure API components are intentional, consistent, and aligned with design guidance.</li>



<li>Guarantee API stability through semantic versioning: teams can adopt components knowing that minor and patch releases will not introduce breaking changes, with breaking changes reserved for major version updates.</li>



<li>Create the @gcds-core namespace as the stable home for packages.</li>



<li>Reflect a new colour scale and added colours to our <a href="https://www.npmjs.com/package/@gcds-core/tokens" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">base design tokens</a>.</li>
</ul>



<p><a href="https://github.com/cds-snc/gcds-components/blob/main/CHANGELOG.md" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Check out the full changelog of code changes</a>.&nbsp;</p>
</details>
